# Control 2.7: Vendor and Third-Party Risk Management

## Overview

**Control ID:** 2.7
**Control Name:** Vendor and Third-Party Risk Management
**Regulatory Reference:** GLBA 501(b), SOX 404, FINRA 4511, OCC 2011-12, Fed SR 11-7, Interagency Third-Party Guidance (2023)
**Setup Time:** 1-2 hours  

---

## Governance Levels

### Baseline (Level 1)

Maintain inventory of third-party connectors and integrations; assess vendor risk.

### Recommended (Level 2-3)

Formal vendor assessment process; annual security questionnaires; SLAs documented.

### Regulated/High-Risk (Level 4)

Comprehensive vendor vetting with contractual security requirements; continuous monitoring; audit access logs.

---

## Verification & Testing

1. Review third-party connector list
2. Check vendor security assessments
3. Verify contracts have required security clauses
4. Confirm vendor access is monitored and logged
EXPECTED: Vendors assessed; security requirements in contracts; access monitored

## Regulatory Context

**Primary Regulations:** GLBA 501(b), SOX 404, FINRA 4511, OCC 2011-12, Fed SR 11-7, Interagency Third-Party Guidance (2023)

This control is required for compliance with the regulations listed above. Financial services organizations subject to these regulations must implement appropriate governance levels based on their risk profile and agent classification (Zone 1, Zone 2, or Zone 3).

### Interagency Third-Party Risk Management Guidance (June 2023)

The joint OCC, Federal Reserve, and FDIC guidance establishes the current standard for third-party risk management at banking organizations. Key requirements include:

- **Planning:** Sound planning for third-party relationships
- **Due Diligence:** Comprehensive assessment before engagement
- **Contract Negotiation:** Appropriate contractual protections
- **Ongoing Monitoring:** Continuous oversight throughout the relationship
- **Termination:** Planning for relationship end

For AI agents utilizing third-party connectors, APIs, or data services, this guidance applies to vendor assessment, contract requirements, and ongoing monitoring activities.
